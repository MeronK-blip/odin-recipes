Persian Rice

Description

Persian Rice is a traditional dish made with basmati rice cooked to fluffy perfection and layered over a bed of thinly sliced potatoes seasoned with cumin and salt. The rice is steamed with butter, saffron, and olive oil, creating distinct, aromatic grains topped with a golden crust of tender, crispy potatoes. The saffron-infused rice is gently mixed and served beautifully with the potatoes lining the edges, offering a combination of fluffy texture, subtle spice, and a mild buttery flavor.

Ingredients

  • 3 quarts water
  • 3 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 russet potato, cut into 1/4-inch slices
  • salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons butter, cut into thin slices, or to taste
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons hot water
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley, or to taste

Steps

  1. Place water and kosher salt in a pot; bring to a boil.
  2. Add rice; cook and stir for exactly 7 minutes. Drain.
  3. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium-high heat.
  4. Cover bottom of the pot with 1 layer of potato slices, sprinkle cumin and salt over potatoes.
  5. Cook until potatoes are sizzling, 2 to 3 minutes; top potatoes with rice to form an even layer.
  6. Reduce heat to low and place butter slices over rice.
  7. Top the pot with a layer of clean paper towels, then place the lid over the towels; steam until rice is fluffy, about 45 minutes.
  8. Grind saffron threads with a mortar and pestle.
  9. Mix crushed saffron with 1 Â½ tablespoons hot water in a large bowl.
  10. Add a couple of spoonfuls of rice to saffron mixture and stir until rice is yellow.
  11. Spoon remaining rice into a serving bowl.
  12. Top with saffron rice, line the edges of the bowl with potatoes, and garnish with parsley.
